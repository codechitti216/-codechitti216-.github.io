Project: Enhanced Intrusion Detection IoT

Abstract

Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) are tools used for security purposes to monitor and analyze network traffic and system activity. Their main goal is to detect any potential malicious activities or policy violations in real time. With the continuous expansion of computer networks, concerns regarding vulnerability and security have become more significant. This has led to the need for effective intrusion detection systems (IDS). However, commercially available IDS often struggle with identifying new types of attacks and may generate false alarms for legitimate user activities. To address these challenges and improve accuracy, we propose a unique approach that combines correlation-based feature selection (CFS) with neural networks for anomaly detection in IDS. To validate our approach, we conducted experimental analysis using benchmark datasets specifically designed for intrusion detection; NSL KDD and UNSW NB. These datasets cover various attack scenarios. Our results demonstrate that our proposed model outperforms several state-of-the-art techniques in terms of accuracy, sensitivity and specificity. By leveraging neural networks along with correlation-based attribute selection, we achieve higher accuracy in identifying anomalies while distinguishing them from legitimate user activities. Moreover, our research highlights the potential application of such IDS models in securing Internet of Things (IoT) servers in the future. This would help in creating strong security measures for wireless payment systems and allow for secure management of interconnected networks, resulting in smooth operations without errors and enhanced performance. In conclusion, our study showcases the efficacy of integrating correlation-based feature selection with neural networks for enhancing anomaly detection in intrusion detection systems. The proposed model exhibits superior performance compared to existing techniques, offering promising prospects for securing computer networks and IoT infrastructures in the face of evolving threats.
