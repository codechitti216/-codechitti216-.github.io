Predicting Early Career Salaries of Engineering Graduates: A Machine Learning Approach

Abstract of the Project

The Indian markets economic sector is characterized by the nation's growth in various sectors during the 1990s. This growth generated a huge demand for trained labor in the job markets, which in turn made engineering one of the top study choices over the years after high school. Compensation planning is another major strategic zone for the growth and performance of companies in the IT industry, where awareness is a key factor for a business. Many companies and corporations consider employee turnover to be a major issue. The issue is important because it affects not only the profitability of work but also the continuity of planning and culture of enterprises. The purpose of this paper is to predict the salary based on skills known to the worker and the work experience he has done in that area. Problem Definition

The problem is to develop a machine learning model that accurately predicts the salaries of engineering students right after they graduate from college. This prediction will help students make informed decisions about their career paths, assist educational institutions in aligning their curriculum with industry demands, and facilitate fair salary negotiations. By leveraging machine learning algorithms and relevant features, we aim to build a robust model that can provide accurate salary estimates based on students' profiles, such as their educational background, specialization, internships, academic performance, and other relevant factors.

Challenges Faced

  • Availability of limited or incomplete data
  • The complexity and variability of the factors that influence engineering salaries. Various factors, such as industry sector, geographic location, job role, company size, and market conditions, can significantly impact salary outcomes
  • The model's generalizability to new and unseen data is a challenge. Engineering salaries can be influenced by external factors such as economic conditions, industry trends, and technological advancements. These factors may change over time, and the model needs to be robust enough to adapt and provide accurate predictions in dynamic environments.

What is Correlation?

What is Correlation? Correlation is a statistical measure used to evaluate the relationship between two variables. It provides insights into how changes in one variable are associated with changes in another variable. The correlation coefficient, ranging from -1 to +1, quantifies the strength and direction of this relationship. A positive correlation indicates that as one variable increases, the other variable tends to increase as well. Conversely, a negative correlation suggests that as one variable increases, the other variable tends to decrease. A correlation coefficient close to 0 implies a weak or no relationship between the variables. It's important to note that correlation does not imply Correlation Matrix Analysis causation, as it solely captures statistical association. Scatter plots are commonly used to visualize correlations. Understanding correlations is crucial in fields such as finance, economics, social sciences, and data analysis, as it aids in identifying patterns, making predictions, and informing decision-making processes.

Machine Learning Models Used

Decision Trees

Decision trees are versatile and interpretable machine learning models that can be used for both classification and regression tasks. They mimic human decision-making processes by recursively partitioning the data based on features to make predictions or decisions.

Linear Regression

Linear regression is a popular and widely used statistical model for typical regression problems.

Random Forest

Random Forest is an ensemble learning method that combines multiple decision trees to make predictions. It is a versatile and powerful algorithm that can be used for both classification and regression tasks.
